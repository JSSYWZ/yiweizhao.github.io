What is a Bulk Purchase Annuity (BPA)?

Key Features

  • Purpose: Transfer pension scheme liabilities to insurers to manage longevity risk and investment volatility, in line with PRA regulations
  • Structure: Single premium payment for a group annuity policy covering multiple members
  • Scale: Typically used for defined benefit (DB) schemes with hundreds or thousands of pensioners

How It Works

  • Scheme trustees pay a lump sum to a UK-regulated insurer (e.g., Aviva, Legal & General)
  • Insurer assumes responsibility for paying scheme benefits under FCA rules
  • Benefits transferred via a buy-in or buy-out policy

Key Benefits

  • Removes longevity risk from the sponsor's balance sheet
  • Reduces PPF levy exposure for pension schemes
  • Payments protected by FSCS up to £85,000 per policyholder

Example Scenario

A FTSE 100 company transfers £400 million in DB pension liabilities to a UK insurer. The insurer becomes responsible for paying 5,000 pensioners their monthly benefits, while the company removes the liability from its accounts and stabilises future costs.

Important Considerations

  • Requires FCA/PRA-regulated insurer approval
  • Trustees must follow TPR guidance on risk transfers
  • Pricing reflects gilt yields and UK longevity projections
